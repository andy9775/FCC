Robert Borden: A Canadian lawyer and the eighth prime minister of Canada

Here is a Timeline of Robert Borden's life:

  • June 26, 1854 - born in Grand-Pré, Nova Scotia
  • 1868-1874 - worked as a teacher in Grand-Pré and Matawan, NJ
  • 1874 - articled as a lawyer
  • 1878 - placed first in the Nova Scotia bar examination
  • 1889 - married Laura Bond
  • 1890 - became the junior partner of John P. Chipman in Kentville, Nova Scotia
  • 1893 - successfully argued his first two cases
  • 1896 - became president of the Nova Scotia Barristers' Society
  • 1896 - elected to Parliament in the 1896 federal election as a Conservative after breaking from the Liberal party over Reciprocity
  • 1901 - selected by the Conservative caucus to succeed Sir Charles Tupper as leader of the Conservative Party
  • 1904 - lost his Halifax seat in the federal election
  • 1907 - worked to rebrand the conservative party
  • 1914 - committed Canada to provide half a million soldiers for WWI
  • 1918 - after pledging himself to the women's suffrage movement, he extended women the right to vote in Canda
  • 1918-1920 - Chancellor of McGill University
  • 1920 - retired from office
  • 1924-1930 - Chancellor of Queen's University
  • June 10, 1937 - died
